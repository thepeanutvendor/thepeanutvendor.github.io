NcA: On Virtue and Morality

The purpose of this article is threefold: to discuss the views of Aristole on virtue and morality (contained in the Nicomachean Ethics); to discuss the views of Nietzsche on the same subjects (contained in On the Geneaology of Morals); to subject them to scrutiny, and see who survives. Accordingly, this article has three parts. Aristotle and Nietzsche were chosen for their contrasting views, and for the quality and quantity of their thought. The latter issue is relevant as I intend to make this a multi-part series on different concerns (ethics, politics, and theoretical philosophy).
