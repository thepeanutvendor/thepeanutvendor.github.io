Are human beings merely self-interested, or malevolent/altruistic?

The prominent enlightenment philosophers - Hobbes, Rousseau, Locke - all based their ethics and politics on a theory of human moral character, or nature (a vaguer term). I wanted to test their - and my - assumptions, and figure out what humans are actually like at their moral core, a question we’re more equipped to answer nowadays.
